## Slot Machine System
- Slot machines tend to have several different jackpots, with scaling rewards depending on the symbol
- Most slot machines have a 3x5 formation where there are positions in v and upside down v formation that can pay out for more
- The majority (top 2/3) of the screen should be filled with some sort of animation that has to do with the theme of the machine
- Play button (levers don't really show up in digital slot machines)
- Variable bet sizes
- Skins: slot machines require security of their sensitive parts away from the user. Skins allow this by calculating results in some kind of cloud interface away from the machine and then sending the results to be masked over. It's good to note however that for our purposes, we likely don't need to do this

## Psychology
- The machine is built to keep the player from getting tired. We should have a button that can use the mouse button andd keyboard to play to mitigate physical fatigue
- While the animations on a slot machine are flashy, the other stuff tend to be relatively minimal. This helps prevent eye fatigue
- Slot machines tend to have moving parts that show up periodically to grab attention and keep players engaged
- Variable awards give this anticipation of receiving the big reward, so things like near misses and mini jackpots keep the player hooked
- Making slot machines look like and work similarly to something like "candy crush" does a lot to help players forget that they're gambling and instead think about gaming
- Background music is used to control the ambience of the situation and artificaially encourage people to keep playing

## Legal concerns
- The RTP (return to player) needs to be somewhere around 75-80%
- Once the RNG determines the outcome of the reel, nothing else is allowed to change it
- Errors must be reported immediately
- Must check for age limitation
